Five diffrent places to visit in Japan

Jigokudani Monkey Park gives visitors a unique opportunity to see Japanese Snow Monkeys bathing in natural hot-spring. Vistors can get there by taking Japanese bullet train (shinkansen) and then walking through the Jigokudani forests. Vistors are not allowed to go into the hot springs, but Jigokudani Monkey Park is located between the onsen towns of Shibu and Yudanaka, so you could potentially go bath in hot-spring afterward. If you are a visitors that find money cute or want to visit something unique in Japan, then the monkey park is the place for you to go!

The Hiroshima Peace Memorial Park is devoted to peace memorial facilities. The park's main facility is the Peace Memorial Museum. The museum focus on the day of the bombing and the life of the people of Hiroshima post-bombing. The display of the museum allows the visitors to feel that they should not take peace for granted.

If you are a cat lover, Cat Cafe ねころび is perfect for you. It is a cafe that serves you free drinks, while you are able to play with cats and other games. It is very cute.

One thing Japan is known for is Ramen, so one can’t go to Japan and not eat Ramen. One of the best Ramen places in Japan is Ichiran Shibuya. The price and food quality is great. Also, it is open 24 hours.

Kinkaku-Ji is a Zen Buddhist temple with two floors completely covered in gold leaf. It was built to represent the extravagant Kitayama culture during Yoshimitsu's time. It is one of the most popular buildings in Japan, attracting a large number of visitors annually. It is a very lovely place to visit.
